Introduction¶

This project is an exploratory analysis of over 10,000+ movies in The Movie Database (TMDB) over a span of 55 years between 1960 and 2015. In this analysis we'll explore the trends behind some of the most popular movies and the actors, directors and movies studios behind them.

To kickstart our analysis, we'll import some of the libraries that will be useful in wrangling and visualizing our data.

Research Question 2: What Impact does Popularity have on Movie Performance?¶

According to TMDB, the popularity metric is calculated by algorithms modeled on a different types of user interactions with a movie. For example, the number of views for the day, saves on a watchlist or total votes received etc.

Let's explore the impacts of this popularity metric on some of the most important success metrics of a movie.

Revenue¶

To explore this relationship between we'll use a scatter diagram. Since we'll be plotting a couple of of them, let's define a helper function, ScatterPlot for plotting our scatter diagrams.

In [26]:
def ScatterPlot(df, xVar, yVar, title=None, xlabel=None, ylabel=None):
    '''
    This function plots a scatter diagram of two variables within the same dataframe.
    The plot's title and axis labels defaults to the variable names if no user-defined arguments are passed.
    
    inputs: Dataframe object - df, Dataframe columns - xVar, yVar
    kwargs: Strings - title, xlabel, ylabel
    outputs: Scatter Diagram of xVar and yVar
    '''
    # Plot
    df.plot(kind = 'scatter', x=xVar, y=yVar,  figsize=(14,8), alpha=0.3)
    
    # Clean up and format labels
    xVar = xVar.replace('_', ' ').title()
    yVar = yVar.replace('_', ' ').title()
    
    # Plot title and fallback
    if not title:
        plt.title(f'Relationship between {xVar} and {yVar}', fontsize=14, weight="bold")
    else:
        plt.title(title, fontsize=14, weight="bold")
    
    # Plot y-Axis label and fallback
    if not xlabel:
        plt.xlabel(xVar, fontsize=10, weight="bold")
    else:
        plt.xlabel(xlabel, fontsize=10, weight="bold")
    
    # Plot y-Axis label and fallback
    if not ylabel:
        plt.ylabel(yVar, fontsize=10, weight="bold")
    else:
        plt.ylabel(ylabel, fontsize=10, weight="bold")

Conclusions¶

From our analysis of the TMDB dataset, we can deduce the following insights:

  1. The Drama, Comedy and Thriller genres are the most prolific genres over the 55 year period spanned by this dataset, while the Westerns, TV Movie and Foreign genres are the least prolific.
  2. The Adventure, Animation and Fantasy genres had the highest budgets on average and also grossed the highest revenues on average. This suggests that there seem to be a good correlation between higher movie budgets and box office revenues.
  3. Adventure, Science Fiction and Fantasy are the most popular genres with TMDB users, while Documentary, Foreign and TV Movie are the least popular.
  4. On a yearly basis, the Drama genre dominated the number of movies released topping the charts for 48 years out of the 55-year span of the data.
  5. There is some loose positive correlation between TMDB popularity scores and Movie Revenue.
  6. There is no observable correlation between the popularity score of a movie and how high or low it is rated.

Limitations Of This Analysis¶

  1. The dataset analysed contained a lot of observations that were incomplete or outdated as at the time this analysis was performed.
  2. Only exploratory analysis was carried out on this dataset.
